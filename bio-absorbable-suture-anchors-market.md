<p><p>Bio-absorbable suture anchors are used in orthopedic surgeries to repair soft tissues to bone. There are two types of suture anchors commonly used: knotted and knotless. Knotted suture anchors require a knot to secure the suture to the anchor, while knotless anchors eliminate the need for a knot by utilizing a locking mechanism. Both types offer advantages and disadvantages based on the specific surgical procedure being performed. The choice between knotted and knotless suture anchors depends on the surgeon's preference and the specific needs of the patient.</p></p>
