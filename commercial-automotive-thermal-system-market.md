<p><p>Commercial automotive thermal systems are divided into two main types: Non-Electronically Controlled Thermal Systems and Electronically Controlled Thermal Systems. Non-Electronically Controlled systems rely on mechanical components such as thermostats and fans to regulate temperature, while Electronically Controlled systems use electronic sensors and actuators for more precise temperature control. The Electronically Controlled systems are becoming increasingly popular in modern vehicles due to their efficiency and ability to optimize engine performance.</p></p>
