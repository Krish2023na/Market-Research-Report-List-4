<p><p>Gastroscopy is a procedure used to examine the inside of the esophagus, stomach, and small intestine. There are three main types of gastroscopes used in the market. The electronic gastroscope uses advanced imaging technology to produce high-quality images of the digestive tract. The fiberoptic gastroscope uses fiber optics to transmit images to a screen for examination. The capsule gastroscope is a small, swallowable camera that captures images as it travels through the digestive system. Each type of gastroscope offers its own unique advantages for diagnosing and treating gastrointestinal conditions.</p></p>
