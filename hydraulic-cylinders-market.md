<p><p>Hydraulic cylinders are commonly categorized into two main types: single-acting and double-acting cylinders. Single-acting cylinders operate in one direction, utilizing hydraulic pressure to extend the piston rod, while an external force (such as gravity or a spring) retracts it. On the other hand, double-acting cylinders use hydraulic pressure to both extend and retract the piston rod, offering more control and versatility in various industrial applications. Both types play a vital role in the hydraulic cylinders market, catering to different operational needs and requirements.</p></p>
