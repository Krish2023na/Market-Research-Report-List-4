<p><p>Internal bone fixation plates are used in orthopedic surgeries to stabilize and support broken bones during the healing process. These plates are available in two main types: metal and ceramics. Metal plates, typically made of stainless steel or titanium, are stronger and provide more stability. Ceramics plates are lighter and more biocompatible, but may not be as strong as metal plates. Both types of plates play a crucial role in the internal fixation of bones and are chosen based on the specific needs of the patient and the nature of the injury.</p></p>
