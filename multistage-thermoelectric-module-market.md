<p><p>Multistage thermoelectric modules consist of multiple stages of thermoelectric materials sandwiched between two metal plates. The most common materials used in these modules are bismuth telluride (Bi2Te3), lead telluride (PbTe), and silicon germanium (SiGe). Bi2Te3 is widely used for its high efficiency and stability, PbTe is known for its high operating temperatures, and SiGe offers a good balance between efficiency and cost. Other materials used in multistage thermoelectric modules include skutterudites and half-Heusler alloys, each with their own unique advantages and applications.</p></p>
